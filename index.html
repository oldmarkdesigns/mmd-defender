Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add new team members to the squad?

+

To add new team members, contact your system administrator or use the "Add Member" function in the Team Members section. New members will need to complete their medical assessments and equipment checks before being marked as mission-ready.

Why is my health data not syncing from my Apple Watch?

+

Ensure your Apple Watch is connected to the same Apple ID as your iPhone, and that Apple Health has permission to share data with MMD Defender. Check the Connections page to verify device status and sync settings.

How often should I update mission readiness data?

+

Mission readiness data should be updated at least daily during active operations, and before each mission briefing. Critical health metrics should be updated immediately if there are any changes in team member status.

What does the readiness percentage mean?

+

The readiness percentage is calculated based on multiple factors including physical fitness, equipment status, medical clearance, and training completion. A score above 80% indicates full mission readiness, 60-80% requires attention, and below 60% indicates not mission-ready.

How do I export mission reports?

+

Go to the Records page, select the "Records" tab, and click on any mission report. Use the export function to download reports in PDF or CSV format. All exports are automatically logged for audit purposes.

Can I access MMD Defender offline?

+

Limited offline functionality is available for viewing cached data and basic team information. Full functionality, including data sync and real-time updates, requires an internet connection. Critical mission data is always cached locally for emergency access.

How do I reset my password?

+

Contact your system administrator or IT support team to reset your password. For security reasons, password resets must be verified through official military channels and may require additional authentication.

What devices are compatible with MMD Defender?

+

MMD Defender supports Apple Watch, Oura Ring, Omron blood pressure monitors, and Withings devices. All devices must be compatible with Apple Health for data synchronization. Check the Connections page for a full list of supported devices.

How is my data protected?

+

All data is encrypted using military-grade encryption (AES-256). Data transmission uses secure protocols (TLS 1.3), and all access is logged and monitored. Data is stored in secure, government-approved facilities with strict access controls.

What should I do if I notice incorrect health data?

+

Immediately report any discrepancies to your medical officer and update the data manually if necessary. Incorrect health data can affect mission planning and team safety. All corrections are logged and may require medical verification.
